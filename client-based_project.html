Client-Based Project

Duration: 14 weeks (Fall 2020)

​ Project Type: Group Project (Kedrick Akons, Lauren Lunsford, Amanda Hardy, and Elizabeth Ritter): The ALEK Consulting Team

​ Responsibilities: Design thinking, Research, and Need-finding interviews

​ Tools: Pencil and Paper, Miro, Zoom, Voice Memos, and Google Drive

​ Overview: I’ve worked in a team with 3 others to help our client with project management processes. Our team did preliminary research to better understand the problem that our client was facing. We conducted interviews with employees from various teams. By creating an affinity wall, our team thought about various solutions that we could present to the client towards the end of the semester. An important note: our client has requested to remain anonymous.



The Problem: Our client needed help with optimizing their workflow in the remote setting. They informed us that it is important for them to improve their project management process to help with efficiency and break down silos to ensure that people can find the information that they need with ease. Our client wants to refine their existing process so that they can move forward with better practices.



The Goal: Our goal as a team was to provide useful recommendations to our client that will improve their workflow/project management process.



My Role: I was part of a team that consisted of 4 people including myself for a semester-long project with a client that must remain anonymous. I took part in all stages of the process such as conducting research, interviewing employees, creating an affinity wall, and brainstorming solutions to help our client.



Research: The ALEK Consulting Team conducted individual background research reports in which we synthesized research about our client. These reports covered different topics. My report focused on understanding project management. This encompasses its benefits, how to wok in virtual teams, understanding common mistakes with project management/communication, and ways to improve project management. The research I did helped me get a better understanding of the problem that our client faced. Additionally, it helped me to begin thinking of possible solutions to help the client.



Interviews: The ALEK Consulting Team had the privilege of conducting interviews with eight individuals who contribute to the blogs. These contacts worked in Marketing, Administration, Public Relations, and Web and were selected with the help of our primary contact. These interviews gave us the opportunity to ask questions and to see the workflow in action. We gathered useful data to help us create affinity notes. Then turn those affinity notes into an affinity wall.



Findings: We found that communication is confined to individual teams. There are communication silos across teams that lead to workflow inefficiencies. We also found that there is little enforcement of platform (Trello) usage. Lastly, our team found that Trello is not used in an effective way.



Solutions:
The ALEK Consulting Team provided a variety of recommendations that will be useful to our client based on our contextual analysis. I’ll list a few below.

  • Adopt a centralized communication platform (Slack specifically)
  • Formulate a “Trello Etiquette” by mandating Trello training so that everyone knows how to use Trello and standardize the information put into Trello Cards​
  • Introduce a centralized archive to reduce the duplication of blog content
